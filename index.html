About Me

I am a computer science instructor with a PhD in Computer Networks. I have taught at a number of reputed universities in Pakistan. I started teaching in the annual examination system, then transitioned to teaching under the semester system. As a PhD student, I studied in the quarter system, then we transitioned to the semester system. I've even taught at an affiliated institution under the UK assessment system. I came into teaching by chance in 1998, and found that I loved it. My students appreciated the effort, which encouraged me to keep going.

Recently, I worked in the EdTech industry in the content team for an online learning platform. This experience helped me understand how technology can be used to enhance learning and make education more accessible. This role gave me a different kind of independence and exposure.

Research

Green computing and data center optimization

Cloud computing consumes massive amounts of energy, impacting both operational costs and the environment. My work in this area introduces frameworks to make "geographically distributed" computing more sustainable.

  • RED-BL Framework: I developed Relocate Energy Demand to Better Locations (RED-BL), a framework that exploits the temporal and geographic variation in electricity prices. By dynamically mapping workloads to data centers in regions where power is currently cheaper or greener, we can reduce electricity bills by up to 45%.
  • Workload Relocation & Resource Pruning: Beyond simple mapping, my research explores "resource pruning"—scaling down active infrastructure during low-demand periods—to achieve maximum electricity cost minimization for service providers.

Key Publications

  • RED-BL: Energy solution for loading data centers (INFOCOM 2012)
  • RED-BL: Evaluating dynamic workload relocation for data center networks (Computer Networks 2014)

Cellular and wireless networks

As mobile data demand grows, cellular networks face a "carbon footprint" challenge. My research focuses on making these networks "traffic-aware" to save energy and improve maintenance.

  • Low-Carb Scheme: I proposed Low-Carb, a practical scheme that couples Base Transceiver Station (BTS) power savings with intelligent call hand-offs. By migrating active calls to neighboring stations during low-traffic periods, we can safely put redundant equipment into "sleep mode," significantly reducing annual energy consumption.
  • Machine Learning for Network Health: Using distributed estimation and summary statistics, I developed methods to predict cell states (such as outages) with high accuracy (96%). This reduces the need for expensive manual "drive tests" and enables proactive network maintenance.

Key Publications

  • Low-Carb: A practical scheme for improving energy efficiency in cellular networks (Computer Communications 2016)
  • Cell state prediction through distributed estimation of transmit power (NEW2AN 2019)
  • Electricity cost efficient workload mapping (INFOCOM Workshops 2013

Network measurements

The Internet is a complex "network of networks." My work involves measuring and debugging these systems to ensure they remain stable and secure against evolving threats.

  • SDN Performance & Debugging: Software-Defined Networking (SDN) is the backbone of modern data centers. I conducted case studies on performance debugging for SDN controllers (like OpenDaylight), identifying bottlenecks in latency and throughput to ensure these "network operating systems" can scale effectively.
  • Internet Evolution (BGP): I track the long-term evolution of the Border Gateway Protocol (BGP) in specific regional contexts, such as South Asia. This research helps us understand how routing stability and geopolitical shifts affect regional internet connectivity over decades.

Key Publications

  • Performance debugging in SDN controllers—a case study (Cloudnet 2016)
  • Ten Years of Event-Driven BGP Evolution in India and Bangladesh (AINTEC 2025)
  • A comparative study of failover schemes for IaaS recovery (ICOIN 2018)

Teaching

University Courses

I've taught a large number and variety of courses in my teaching career. The two courses that I am currently teaching at LUMS are described below.

  • CS 200 Introduction to Programming:This course focuses on a review of procedural programming in C and a transition to object-oriented programming (OOP) in C++. We start with a review of pointers, cover double pointers, recursion, basic tenets of OOP, classes, objects, inheritance, composition, aggregation, association, polymorphism, and templates. We also cover key containers and algorithms from the C++ standard templates library (STL).
  • CS 4602 Coding for Careers: This course focuses on coding interview preparation. We describe the typical prevalent software engineering interview process, then cover various patterns for solving coding interview problems. Students solve selected problems on LeetCode. We also cover system design interview preparation. The course also has invited talks by practictioners.
